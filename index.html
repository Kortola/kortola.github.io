About the Artist

I began painting in São Paulo, Brazil, where I studied fine arts at the Escola Panamericana de Arte. The city was my first subject: urban, colorful, chaotic. I painted its energy on large canvases in acrylics, finding my voice as a painter in the intensity of one of the world's great metropolitan landscapes. Moving to Indiana changed everything. Surrounded by open skies, still water, and the unhurried rhythm of a small Midwestern city, I discovered what I had been looking for all along: quiet. The chaos gave way to calm, the urban density to open horizon, the noise to light. Today I paint the landscapes that surround me, the places I have visited, and the quiet moments of everyday life. Always in acrylics, always beginning with light, always with the same intention: to paint something that makes people stop, breathe, and feel at peace. My relationship with light and color runs deeper than technique. My father was born in Valencia, Spain, the homeland of Joaquín Sorolla, the great painter of Mediterranean light. I discovered Sorolla's work as an artist, not knowing at first how personally it would resonate. I deeply admire his fearless use of color, his expressive and confident brushstrokes, his treatment of light as the true subject of every painting, and his beautiful interpretation of quotidian scenes. His work guides and inspires my own approach to painting in ways I am still discovering. The connection felt immediate and deeply personal. People who see my paintings often tell me they feel calm just looking at them. That is the highest thing I could hope to achieve: that someone might carry a little more stillness into their day because of it.
